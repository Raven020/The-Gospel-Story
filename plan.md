# Jesus JRPG — Story & Timeline Plan

## Game Concept
A web-based retro JRPG in the style of Pokemon/early Final Fantasy.
The player controls Jesus (and companions) through a narrative adventure
spanning his public ministry, death, and resurrection.
Gameplay will involve exploration, dialogue trees, turn-based encounters
(spiritual battles), and story-driven quests.

---

## STORY ARCS & TIMELINE

---

### ARC 1 — THE BOY JESUS (Luke 2:41–52)
**Location:** Jerusalem / Temple of Jerusalem

**Events:**
1. Joseph and Mary travel to Jerusalem for Passover with 12-year-old Jesus.
2. After the festival, the family begins the journey home.
3. Jesus is discovered missing after a day of travel.
4. Mary and Joseph return to Jerusalem and search for three days.
5. They find Jesus in the Temple courts, sitting among the teachers (Pharisees/scribes).
6. Jesus is listening and asking questions; the teachers are amazed at his understanding.
7. Mary confronts him: *"Son, why have you treated us like this?"*
8. Jesus responds: *"Didn't you know I had to be in my Father's house?"*
9. He returns to Nazareth with them and grows in wisdom and stature.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Tutorial arc. Player controls Mary and Joseph (not Jesus).
- Explore Jerusalem, talk to NPCs, solve the "find the boy" puzzle.
- Simplified encounter system (NPC dialogue encounters, no full combat). A teacher mentions a boy with wondrous knowledge in the Temple.
- Wisdom Q&A witnessed upon finding Jesus in the Temple.
- Ends with a cutscene of Jesus growing up in Nazareth.

---

### ARC 2 — THE BAPTISM & TEMPTATION (Matthew 3–4, Mark 1, Luke 3–4)
**Location:** Jordan River → Judean Wilderness

**Events:**
1. John the Baptist preaches repentance and baptizes in the Jordan.
2. Jesus comes to be baptized; John protests, Jesus insists.
3. The heavens open — the Holy Spirit descends like a dove.
4. God's voice: *"This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased."*
5. Jesus is led by the Spirit into the wilderness for 40 days.
6. Satan appears and presents three temptations:
   - **Temptation 1:** Turn stones into bread (physical need/power).
   - **Temptation 2:** Throw yourself from the Temple pinnacle (test God).
   - **Temptation 3:** Bow to me and receive all the kingdoms of the world (glory).
7. Jesus rebukes Satan each time with Scripture.
8. Satan departs; angels come and minister to Jesus.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- John the Baptist joins as an NPC guide.
- Baptism triggers a visual transformation/power-up event.
- Wilderness = first dungeon. Three boss-style encounters with Satan using Scripture as the combat mechanic (choose the right verse to counter each temptation).

---

### ARC 3 — CALLING OF THE DISCIPLES (Matthew 4, Mark 1–3, Luke 5–6, John 1)
**Location:** Sea of Galilee / Capernaum

**Events:**
1. Jesus begins preaching: *"Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near."*
2. Calls Simon (Peter) and Andrew from their fishing nets.
3. Calls James and John (sons of Zebedee) from their boat.
4. Calls Philip and Nathanael (John 1:43–51).
5. Calls Matthew (Levi) the tax collector (Matthew 9:9).
6. Choosing of all Twelve Apostles on a mountainside (Luke 6:12–16):
   - Peter, Andrew, James, John, Philip, Bartholomew,
     Matthew, Thomas, James son of Alphaeus, Thaddaeus,
     Simon the Zealot, Judas Iscariot.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Each disciple recruited = a new party member with unique abilities.
- Peter: strength/fishing nets. Matthew: gold/money abilities. Thomas: doubt/detection skill.
- Judas joins with a hidden "betrayal" stat the player doesn't know about yet.

---

### ARC 4 — EARLY MIRACLES & MINISTRY (John 2–4, Luke 4–5)
**Location:** Cana / Capernaum / Samaria / Nazareth

**Events:**
1. **Wedding at Cana** — Jesus turns water into wine at his mother's request. First miracle.
2. **Cleansing the Temple** — Jesus overturns the money changers' tables in Jerusalem.
3. **Nicodemus visits at night** — Jesus explains being "born again" (John 3:1–21).
4. **Woman at the Well (Samaria)** — Jesus reveals himself to a Samaritan woman; she believes and tells her village (John 4:1–42).
5. **Rejection at Nazareth** — Jesus reads Isaiah in his hometown synagogue; *"A prophet is not accepted in his hometown."* The crowd tries to throw him off a cliff; he walks through them (Luke 4:16–30).
6. **Miraculous catch of fish** — Peter's net nearly breaks; he falls at Jesus' feet (Luke 5:1–11).

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Cana: resource transformation mini-game.
- Temple: crowd/chase sequence.
- Woman at the Well: dialogue quest unlocking the Samaria region.
- Nazareth: stealth/escape event.

---

### ARC 5 — THE SERMON ON THE MOUNT & TEACHINGS (Matthew 5–7, Luke 6)
**Location:** Hillside near the Sea of Galilee

**Events:**
1. The Beatitudes: *"Blessed are the poor in spirit... the meek... the peacemakers..."*
2. Salt and Light teaching.
3. Fulfillment of the Law (you've heard it said... but I say...).
4. Lord's Prayer taught.
5. Teachings on fasting, money, worry, judging others.
6. The Golden Rule.
7. Build on the rock vs. sand parable.
8. Crowds are amazed — he teaches with authority.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Sermon = a story-heavy cutscene chapter with player choices reflecting the Beatitudes.
- Player choices here affect party morale and character stat bonuses later.

---

### ARC 6 — MIRACLES OF HEALING & POWER (Matthew 8–9, Mark 1–5, Luke 7–8)
**Location:** Galilee / Capernaum / Nain / Sea of Galilee

**Events:**
1. **Healing the Leper** — Jesus touches him; *"Be clean!"*
2. **Centurion's Servant** — Roman soldier's faith amazes Jesus; servant healed from a distance.
3. **Widow's Son at Nain** — Jesus raises a dead young man; crowd glorifies God.
4. **Calming the Storm** — Jesus rebukes the wind and waves; *"Why are you so afraid?"*
5. **Healing the Gadarene Demoniac** — Legion of demons cast into a herd of pigs.
6. **Jairus's Daughter** — Jesus raises her from the dead.
7. **Woman with the issue of blood** — She touches the hem of his garment and is healed.
8. **Healing of Two Blind Men** (Matthew 9).
9. **Feeding the 5,000** — Five loaves and two fish feed a crowd (all four Gospels).
10. **Walking on Water** — Jesus walks on the Sea of Galilee; Peter attempts it and sinks (Matthew 14).

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Healing encounters = puzzle/dialogue events.
- Storm = ship navigation mini-game.
- Gadarene demoniac = major spiritual battle dungeon encounter.
- Feeding 5,000 = resource management event.
- Walking on water = platformer mini-sequence; Peter's faith meter depletes.

### ARC 7 — PARABLES & DEEPER TEACHINGS (Matthew 13, Mark 4, Luke 10–15)
**Location:** Various — fields, synagogues, homes

**Key Parables:**
1. **The Sower** — Seed on different soils. (Understanding = unlocking regions.)
2. **The Prodigal Son** — Father runs to meet returning son.
3. **The Good Samaritan** — Who is my neighbor? (Side quest in Samaria.)
4. **The Lost Sheep & Lost Coin.**
5. **The Rich Fool.**
6. **The Pharisee and the Tax Collector.**
7. **The Talents** — Investing what you're given.
8. **Lazarus and the Rich Man.**
9. **The Wedding Banquet.**
10. **The Mustard Seed & Leaven.**

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Each major parable = optional side quest or world event.
- Good Samaritan = a quest where the player chooses to help or pass by a wounded NPC.
- Prodigal Son = NPC companion backstory mission.

---

### ARC 8 — TRANSFIGURATION (Matthew 17, Mark 9, Luke 9)
**Location:** A High Mountain (traditionally Mount Tabor or Mount Hermon)

**Events:**
1. Jesus takes Peter, James, and John up a mountain.
2. Jesus is transfigured — his face shines like the sun, his clothes become white as light.
3. Moses and Elijah appear and speak with Jesus.
4. Peter offers to build three shelters.
5. A bright cloud covers them; God speaks: *"This is my Son, whom I love; listen to him!"*
6. The disciples fall face down in terror.
7. Jesus touches them: *"Get up. Don't be afraid."*
8. Coming down, Jesus tells them to tell no one until after the resurrection.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Mountain dungeon climb.
- Transfiguration = massive visual transformation event/cutscene.
- Moses and Elijah appear as legendary NPC guests.
- Boss battle hint dropped about "what is to come."

---

### ARC 9 — JOURNEY TO JERUSALEM & LATER MINISTRY (Luke 9–19, John 7–12)
**Location:** Judea / Perea / Bethany / Jerusalem

**Events:**
1. **Mary, Martha, and Lazarus** — Jesus visits their home; Mary sits at his feet (Luke 10).
2. **The Rich Young Ruler** — *"Go, sell everything you have and give to the poor."* He walks away sad.
3. **Zacchaeus the Tax Collector** — Jesus calls him down from a sycamore tree; salvation comes to his house (Luke 19:1–10).
4. **Healing of Blind Bartimaeus** — *"What do you want me to do for you?"* (Mark 10:46–52).
5. **Raising of Lazarus** — Jesus raises Lazarus from the dead after four days (John 11). *"I am the resurrection and the life."*
6. **Mary anoints Jesus** — Mary pours expensive perfume on Jesus' feet at Bethany (John 12). Judas objects.
7. **The Triumphal Entry** — Jesus rides a donkey into Jerusalem; crowds wave palm branches and shout *"Hosanna!"* (All four Gospels).

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Lazarus = major story dungeon — tomb exploration, raising the dead as a dramatic event.
- Triumphal Entry = parade/escort sequence through Jerusalem map.
- Zacchaeus quest = hidden NPC in a tree; dialogue unlock event.
- Rich Young Ruler = moral choice event affecting player's "treasure in heaven" stat.

---

### ARC 10 — TEMPLE CONFRONTATIONS & LAST TEACHINGS (Matthew 21–25, Mark 11–13, Luke 19–21)
**Location:** Jerusalem / Temple Mount

**Events:**
1. **Second Cleansing of the Temple** — Tables overturned again: *"My house will be called a house of prayer."*
2. **Fig Tree Cursed** — The withered fig tree; lesson on faith.
3. **Authority Challenged** — Pharisees, Sadducees, and scribes try to trap Jesus with questions:
   - Paying taxes to Caesar.
   - The resurrection (whose wife will she be?).
   - The greatest commandment: *"Love God... love your neighbor."*
4. **Woes to the Pharisees** — Seven woes pronounced (Matthew 23).
5. **The Widow's Mite** — She gives two small coins; *"She put in more than all the others."*
6. **The Olivet Discourse** — Jesus predicts the destruction of the Temple, signs of the end times, and his return.
7. **Parable of the Ten Virgins** — Be ready; you don't know the hour.
8. **Parable of the Talents** (Matthew 25).
9. **The Sheep and the Goats** — Final judgment described.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Pharisee confrontations = debate/riddle battles (logic/scripture combat).
- Widow's Mite = a moment of reflection that boosts all party stats.
- Olivet Discourse = major prophecy cutscene, hints at endgame.

---

### ARC 11 — THE LAST SUPPER (Matthew 26, Mark 14, Luke 22, John 13–17)
**Location:** Upper Room, Jerusalem

**Events:**
1. Preparations made; a man carrying a water jar leads them to the upper room.
2. **Foot Washing** — Jesus washes the disciples' feet (John 13:1–17). *"No servant is greater than his master."*
3. **Announcement of the Betrayer** — Jesus dips bread and gives it to Judas. *"What you are about to do, do quickly."* Judas leaves.
4. **The Lord's Supper Instituted** — Bread = his body. Cup = new covenant in his blood.
5. **Peter's Denial Predicted** — *"Before the rooster crows, you will disown me three times."*
6. **Farewell Discourse (John 14–16):**
   - *"I am the way, the truth, and the life."*
   - Promise of the Holy Spirit (Counselor/Comforter).
   - *"Greater love has no one than this: to lay down one's life for one's friends."*
   - The vine and branches.
   - *"In this world you will have trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world."*
7. **High Priestly Prayer (John 17)** — Jesus prays for himself, the disciples, and all future believers.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Upper Room = intimate party hub; manage companion relationships.
- Judas's "betrayal" stat becomes visible; he leaves the party.
- Lord's Supper = spiritual stat restoration event.
- Farewell Discourse = major story cutscene chapter with emotional weight.

### ARC 12 — GETHSEMANE & ARREST (Matthew 26, Mark 14, Luke 22, John 18)
**Location:** Garden of Gethsemane, Mount of Olives

**Events:**
1. Jesus and disciples cross the Kidron Valley to the garden.
2. Jesus takes Peter, James, and John deeper in; asks them to keep watch.
3. Jesus prays in anguish: *"Father, if it is possible, may this cup be taken from me. Yet not as I will, but as you will."*
4. An angel appears and strengthens him; his sweat is like drops of blood.
5. Three times he returns to find the disciples asleep.
6. Judas arrives with soldiers, chief priests, and Pharisees carrying torches and weapons.
7. Judas betrays Jesus with a kiss.
8. Peter cuts off Malchus's ear; Jesus heals it.
9. Jesus is arrested; disciples flee.
10. A young man (possibly Mark) runs away naked.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Gethsemane = stealth/tension dungeon. Player must keep disciples awake (mini-game).
- Betrayal cutscene = emotional story beat. Judas boss encounter triggers.
- Peter's sword attack = failed combat event; Jesus heals the enemy (unique mechanic).
- Arrest = unavoidable; party scatters. Solo Jesus segment begins.

### ARC 13 — TRIALS (Matthew 26–27, Mark 14–15, Luke 22–23, John 18–19)
**Location:** Annas's House → Caiaphas → Pilate → Herod → Pilate

**Events:**
1. **Before Annas** — Informal questioning; Jesus struck by a guard.
2. **Before Caiaphas & the Sanhedrin** — Night trial. False witnesses. *"Are you the Messiah?"* Jesus: *"I am."* Charged with blasphemy. Mocked, struck, spat upon.
3. **Peter's Three Denials** — By a fire in the courtyard. The rooster crows. Peter weeps bitterly.
4. **Before Pilate (First)** — *"Are you the King of the Jews?"* Pilate finds no fault.
5. **Before Herod Antipas** — Jesus silent; Herod mocks him, puts on a gorgeous robe, sends him back.
6. **Before Pilate (Second)** — Pilate offers Barabbas or Jesus. Crowd shouts *"Crucify him!"* Pilate washes his hands.
7. **Judas's Remorse & Death** — Judas returns the 30 pieces of silver and hangs himself (Matthew 27:3–10).
8. **Jesus Flogged and Mocked** — Crown of thorns, purple robe, *"Hail, King of the Jews!"*

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Trial sequence = dialogue-only chapters; no combat, pure narrative tension.
- Peter's denials = heartbreaking optional-view cutscenes.
- Player watches as crowd chooses Barabbas — moral choice felt but not controlled.
- Herod chapter = brief dark comedy sequence of mockery.

---

### ARC 14 — THE CRUCIFIXION (Matthew 27, Mark 15, Luke 23, John 19)
**Location:** Via Dolorosa → Golgotha (Place of the Skull)

**Events:**
1. Jesus is led out carrying his cross.
2. Simon of Cyrene compelled to carry the cross.
3. Women of Jerusalem weep; Jesus speaks to them.
4. Arrived at Golgotha. Wine mixed with myrrh offered; Jesus refuses.
5. Jesus is crucified between two thieves. *"Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing."*
6. Soldiers divide his garments and cast lots.
7. The sign: "JESUS OF NAZARETH, KING OF THE JEWS" in Hebrew, Latin, and Greek.
8. Passersby mock him. Chief priests mock. One thief mocks; the other asks Jesus to remember him. *"Today you will be with me in paradise."*
9. Mary (mother), Mary Magdalene, and John stand at the foot of the cross. Jesus entrusts Mary to John.
10. Darkness from noon to 3 pm.
11. *"My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?"* (Psalm 22)
12. *"I am thirsty."* Sponge of sour wine.
13. *"It is finished."* Jesus bows his head and gives up his spirit.
14. The Temple curtain tears from top to bottom. Earthquake. Tombs open.
15. Centurion: *"Surely this was the Son of God."*
16. Soldier pierces Jesus's side — blood and water flow.
17. Joseph of Arimathea requests the body from Pilate.
18. Jesus buried in Joseph's new tomb with Nicodemus's help; stone rolled across entrance.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Via Dolorosa = somber walking sequence with dialogue events along the path.
- Crucifixion = non-combat chapter; pure storytelling and atmosphere.
- Seven Last Words presented as pivotal dialogue moments.
- Temple curtain tearing = visual world-event affecting all maps (darkness, trembling).
- Burial = end of Act II; dark "game over" style screen before resurrection.

### ARC 15 — THE RESURRECTION (Matthew 28, Mark 16, Luke 24, John 20)
**Location:** The Tomb → Jerusalem → Emmaus

**Events:**
1. **The Empty Tomb (Sunday morning)** — Mary Magdalene and others go to the tomb. The stone is rolled away. An angel: *"He is not here; he has risen!"*
2. **Tomb Guards Report** — Soldiers bribed to say disciples stole the body.
3. **Jesus Appears to Mary Magdalene** — She thinks he's the gardener. *"Mary!"* She recognizes him. *"Rabboni!"* He sends her to tell the disciples.
4. **Road to Emmaus** — Two disciples walk with a stranger; Jesus explains the Scriptures. Recognized at the breaking of bread; he vanishes (Luke 24:13–35).
5. **Jesus Appears to the Disciples** — Appears behind locked doors. *"Peace be with you!"* Shows his hands and side. Breathes on them: *"Receive the Holy Spirit."*
6. **Thomas's Doubt** — Thomas wasn't there; refuses to believe. Eight days later, Jesus appears again. *"Stop doubting and believe."* Thomas: *"My Lord and my God!"*
7. **Appearance by the Sea of Galilee** — Disciples fishing catch nothing; a man on shore tells them to cast on the right side — massive catch. Peter leaps into the water.
8. **Reinstatement of Peter** — Three times: *"Do you love me?"* Three times: *"Feed my sheep."*
9. **Various Appearances** — To James, to more than 500 at once (1 Corinthians 15:6).

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Resurrection = new game world tone; everything brighter, enemies changed.
- Mary Magdalene scene = emotional reunion cutscene.
- Road to Emmaus = travel quest where the stranger NPC joins, then disappears.
- Thomas = companion quest; his "doubt" stat must be resolved via the encounter.
- Peter's reinstatement = three-question dialogue that mirrors his three denials.
- Fishing scene = final relaxed fishing mini-game before the endgame.

---

### ARC 16 — THE GREAT COMMISSION & ASCENSION (Matthew 28, Mark 16, Luke 24, Acts 1)
**Location:** Galilee → Jerusalem → Mount of Olives (Bethany)

**Events:**
1. **The Great Commission (Matthew 28:16–20)** — Jesus meets the eleven on a mountain in Galilee.
   - *"All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me."*
   - *"Go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."*
   - *"And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age."*
2. **Final Instructions in Jerusalem** — Jesus instructs them to wait in Jerusalem for the promised Holy Spirit (Acts 1:4–5).
   - *"You will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth."*
3. **The Ascension (Acts 1:9–11)** — On the Mount of Olives:
   - Jesus is taken up into heaven while the disciples watch.
   - A cloud hides him from their sight.
   - Two men in white appear: *"Men of Galilee, why do you stand here looking into the sky? This same Jesus, who has been taken from you into heaven, will come back in the same way you have seen him go into heaven."*
   - Disciples return to Jerusalem with great joy.

**JRPG Gameplay Hook:**
- Great Commission = final quest log issued to the player; endgame objective unlocked.
- Ascension = grand final cutscene; Jesus rises into light.
- Two angels deliver final dialogue (closing narration).
- Post-credits scene hints at Pentecost (Acts 2) — sequel hook.
- Credits roll over the disciples returning to Jerusalem.

## NOTES FOR GAME DESIGN
- Total story arcs: **16**
- Acts 2 (Pentecost) can serve as a post-credits scene or DLC/sequel.
- Each Arc can be a self-contained "chapter" or "town + dungeon" pair in JRPG structure.
- Route system connects locations (Pokemon-style paths with encounters, NPCs, and items between towns/dungeons).
- Random encounters: Pokemon tall-grass style on routes and dungeons. Frequency to be tuned.
- Enemy types: demons/evil spirits, abstract spiritual forces (Doubt, Fear, Pride, etc.), and healing encounters.
- Active party: 5 (Jesus + 4 disciples). Pokemon-style.
- Scripture references should be subtly displayed in the UI as flavor text.
- The game should remain reverent and faithful to the biblical text.
- Gameplay mechanics (battles, puzzles) are metaphorical — representing spiritual conflict,
  faith, and ministry rather than literal violence.
