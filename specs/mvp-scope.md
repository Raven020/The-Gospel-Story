# MVP Scope — Arcs 1-3

## Purpose
Build Arcs 1-3 as a playable demo to dial in the art style, core gameplay mechanics, and engine foundation before expanding to the full 16-arc game.

## Included Arcs

### Arc 1 — The Boy Jesus (Luke 2:41-52)
- **Location:** Jerusalem / Temple of Jerusalem
- **Player character:** Mary and Joseph (not Jesus)
- **Gameplay:** Tutorial arc — exploration, NPC dialogue, "find the boy" puzzle
- **Encounters:** Simplified NPC dialogue encounters (no full combat). A teacher mentions a boy with wondrous knowledge in the Temple as a breadcrumb.
- **Mini-game:** Temple wisdom Q&A witnessed upon finding Jesus (not player-controlled combat)
- **Ends with:** Cutscene of Jesus growing up in Nazareth

### Arc 2 — The Baptism & Temptation (Matthew 3-4, Mark 1, Luke 3-4)
- **Location:** Jordan River, Judean Wilderness
- **Gameplay:** John the Baptist as NPC guide, baptism visual event, wilderness dungeon
- **Mini-game:** Scripture-selection boss fights — three encounters with Satan
- **Key moment:** Baptism transformation/power-up event

### Arc 3 — Calling of the Disciples (Matthew 4, Mark 1-3, Luke 5-6, John 1)
- **Location:** Sea of Galilee / Capernaum
- **Gameplay:** Disciple recruitment — each recruit adds a new party member with unique abilities
- **Key moment:** Building the initial party roster

## MVP Systems to Build
1. Custom WebGL rendering engine (tilemaps, sprites, animation)
2. Responsive display scaling
3. Top-down overworld exploration and movement
4. NPC dialogue system with dialogue trees
5. Traditional JRPG turn-based combat (spiritually themed)
6. Scripture-selection combat (for Satan boss fights)
7. Wisdom Q&A / debate mini-game
8. Party management (recruit, swap, view stats)
9. Free save/load system
10. Cutscene / narrative event system
11. Tilemap and world loading

## Out of Scope for MVP
- Full 12-disciple roster (only early recruits: Peter, Andrew, James, John, Philip, Nathanael)
- Parables side quests
- Advanced mini-games (fishing, stealth, platforming, resource management)
- Morale system (can be stubbed but not fully implemented)
