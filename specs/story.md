# Story Arcs & Timeline

## Game Concept
A web-based retro JRPG in the style of Pokemon/early Final Fantasy.
The player controls Jesus (and companions) through a narrative adventure
spanning his public ministry, death, and resurrection.
Gameplay involves exploration, dialogue trees, turn-based encounters
(spiritual battles), and story-driven quests.

---

## ARC 1 — THE BOY JESUS (Luke 2:41-52)
**Location:** Jerusalem / Temple of Jerusalem

**Events:**
1. Joseph and Mary travel to Jerusalem for Passover with 12-year-old Jesus.
2. After the festival, the family begins the journey home.
3. Jesus is discovered missing after a day of travel.
4. Mary and Joseph return to Jerusalem and search for three days.
5. They find Jesus in the Temple courts, sitting among the teachers (Pharisees/scribes).
6. Jesus is listening and asking questions; the teachers are amazed at his understanding.
7. Mary confronts him: *"Son, why have you treated us like this?"*
8. Jesus responds: *"Didn't you know I had to be in my Father's house?"*
9. He returns to Nazareth with them and grows in wisdom and stature.

**Gameplay:** Tutorial arc. Player controls Mary and Joseph. Explore Jerusalem, talk to NPCs, solve the "find the boy" puzzle. Simplified encounter system (NPC dialogue encounters, no full combat). A teacher encountered along the way mentions a boy with wondrous knowledge in the Temple, serving as a breadcrumb. Finding Jesus triggers the wisdom Q&A scene as a witnessed event. Ends with cutscene of Jesus growing up.

## ARC 2 — THE BAPTISM & TEMPTATION (Matthew 3-4, Mark 1, Luke 3-4)
**Location:** Jordan River / Judean Wilderness

**Events:**
1. John the Baptist preaches repentance and baptizes in the Jordan.
2. Jesus comes to be baptized; John protests, Jesus insists.
3. The heavens open — the Holy Spirit descends like a dove.
4. God's voice: *"This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased."*
5. Jesus is led by the Spirit into the wilderness for 40 days.
6. Satan appears and presents three temptations:
   - **Temptation 1:** Turn stones into bread (physical need/power).
   - **Temptation 2:** Throw yourself from the Temple pinnacle (test God).
   - **Temptation 3:** Bow to me and receive all the kingdoms of the world (glory).
7. Jesus rebukes Satan each time with Scripture.
8. Satan departs; angels come and minister to Jesus.

**Gameplay:** John the Baptist as NPC guide. Baptism triggers visual transformation/power-up. Wilderness is the first dungeon. Three Scripture-selection boss encounters with Satan.

## ARC 3 — CALLING OF THE DISCIPLES (Matthew 4, Mark 1-3, Luke 5-6, John 1)
**Location:** Sea of Galilee / Capernaum

**Events:**
1. Jesus begins preaching: *"Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near."*
2. Calls Simon (Peter) and Andrew from their fishing nets.
3. Calls James and John (sons of Zebedee) from their boat.
4. Calls Philip and Nathanael (John 1:43-51).
5. Calls Matthew (Levi) the tax collector (Matthew 9:9).
6. Choosing of all Twelve Apostles on a mountainside (Luke 6:12-16).

**Gameplay:** Each disciple recruited = new party member with unique abilities. Judas joins with hidden "betrayal" stat.

---

## ARC 4 — EARLY MIRACLES & MINISTRY (John 2-4, Luke 4-5)
**Location:** Cana / Capernaum / Samaria / Nazareth

**Events:**
1. **Wedding at Cana** — Jesus turns water into wine. First miracle.
2. **Cleansing the Temple** — Jesus overturns the money changers' tables.
3. **Nicodemus visits at night** — Jesus explains being "born again" (John 3:1-21).
4. **Woman at the Well (Samaria)** — Jesus reveals himself to a Samaritan woman (John 4:1-42).
5. **Rejection at Nazareth** — *"A prophet is not accepted in his hometown."* The crowd tries to throw him off a cliff; he walks through them (Luke 4:16-30).
6. **Miraculous catch of fish** — Peter's net nearly breaks (Luke 5:1-11).

**Gameplay:** Resource transformation mini-game (Cana). Temple crowd/chase sequence. Dialogue quest (Woman at the Well). Stealth/escape event (Nazareth).

---

## ARC 5 — THE SERMON ON THE MOUNT (Matthew 5-7, Luke 6)
**Location:** Hillside near the Sea of Galilee

**Events:**
1. The Beatitudes.
2. Salt and Light teaching.
3. Fulfillment of the Law.
4. Lord's Prayer taught.
5. Teachings on fasting, money, worry, judging others.
6. The Golden Rule.
7. Build on the rock vs. sand parable.
8. Crowds are amazed — he teaches with authority.

**Gameplay:** Story-heavy cutscene chapter with player choices reflecting the Beatitudes. Choices affect party morale and stat bonuses.

## ARC 6 — MIRACLES OF HEALING & POWER (Matthew 8-9, Mark 1-5, Luke 7-8)
**Location:** Galilee / Capernaum / Nain / Sea of Galilee

**Events:**
1. Healing the Leper.
2. Centurion's Servant healed from a distance.
3. Widow's Son at Nain raised from the dead.
4. Calming the Storm.
5. Healing the Gadarene Demoniac (Legion).
6. Jairus's Daughter raised.
7. Woman with the issue of blood healed.
8. Healing of Two Blind Men.
9. Feeding the 5,000.
10. Walking on Water — Peter attempts it and sinks.

**Gameplay:** Healing = puzzle/dialogue events. Storm = ship navigation. Gadarene demoniac = major dungeon. Feeding 5,000 = resource management. Walking on water = platformer mini-sequence with Peter's faith meter.

## ARC 7 — PARABLES & DEEPER TEACHINGS (Matthew 13, Mark 4, Luke 10-15)
**Location:** Various — fields, synagogues, homes

**Key Parables:**
1. The Sower.
2. The Prodigal Son.
3. The Good Samaritan.
4. The Lost Sheep & Lost Coin.
5. The Rich Fool.
6. The Pharisee and the Tax Collector.
7. The Talents.
8. Lazarus and the Rich Man.
9. The Wedding Banquet.
10. The Mustard Seed & Leaven.

**Gameplay:** Each major parable = optional side quest or world event. Good Samaritan = help-or-pass choice quest. Prodigal Son = companion backstory mission.

## ARC 8 — TRANSFIGURATION (Matthew 17, Mark 9, Luke 9)
**Location:** A High Mountain

**Events:**
1. Jesus takes Peter, James, and John up a mountain.
2. Jesus is transfigured — face shines like the sun, clothes become white as light.
3. Moses and Elijah appear.
4. Peter offers to build three shelters.
5. God speaks: *"This is my Son, whom I love; listen to him!"*
6. Disciples fall in terror; Jesus reassures them.
7. Descending, Jesus tells them to tell no one until after the resurrection.

**Gameplay:** Mountain dungeon climb (party locked to Peter, James, John). Transfiguration = major visual event/cutscene. Moses and Elijah as legendary NPC guests.

---

## ARC 9 — JOURNEY TO JERUSALEM (Luke 9-19, John 7-12)
**Location:** Judea / Perea / Bethany / Jerusalem

**Events:**
1. Mary, Martha, and Lazarus visited (Luke 10).
2. The Rich Young Ruler walks away sad.
3. Zacchaeus the Tax Collector (Luke 19:1-10).
4. Healing of Blind Bartimaeus (Mark 10:46-52).
5. Raising of Lazarus (John 11). *"I am the resurrection and the life."*
6. Mary anoints Jesus at Bethany (John 12). Judas objects.
7. The Triumphal Entry — palm branches, *"Hosanna!"*

**Gameplay:** Lazarus = major story dungeon. Triumphal Entry = parade/escort sequence. Zacchaeus = hidden NPC quest. Rich Young Ruler = moral choice event.

---

## ARC 10 — TEMPLE CONFRONTATIONS (Matthew 21-25, Mark 11-13, Luke 19-21)
**Location:** Jerusalem / Temple Mount

**Events:**
1. Second Cleansing of the Temple.
2. Fig Tree Cursed.
3. Authority Challenged — Pharisees try to trap Jesus with questions.
4. Woes to the Pharisees (Matthew 23).
5. The Widow's Mite.
6. The Olivet Discourse — destruction of the Temple, signs of the end.
7. Parable of the Ten Virgins.
8. Parable of the Talents.
9. The Sheep and the Goats.

**Gameplay:** Pharisee confrontations = debate/riddle battles (Scripture combat). Widow's Mite = stat-boosting reflection moment. Olivet Discourse = prophecy cutscene.

---

## ARC 11 — THE LAST SUPPER (Matthew 26, Mark 14, Luke 22, John 13-17)
**Location:** Upper Room, Jerusalem

**Events:**
1. Preparations; led to the upper room.
2. Foot Washing (John 13:1-17).
3. Announcement of the Betrayer — Judas leaves.
4. The Lord's Supper Instituted.
5. Peter's Denial Predicted.
6. Farewell Discourse (John 14-16).
7. High Priestly Prayer (John 17).

**Gameplay:** Upper Room = intimate party hub. Judas's betrayal stat becomes visible; he leaves the party. Lord's Supper = spiritual stat restoration. Farewell Discourse = major emotional cutscene.

---

## ARC 12 — GETHSEMANE & ARREST (Matthew 26, Mark 14, Luke 22, John 18)
**Location:** Garden of Gethsemane, Mount of Olives

**Events:**
1. Jesus and disciples cross to the garden.
2. Jesus takes Peter, James, and John deeper; asks them to keep watch.
3. Jesus prays in anguish: *"Not as I will, but as you will."*
4. An angel strengthens him; sweat like drops of blood.
5. Three times he finds disciples asleep.
6. Judas arrives with soldiers.
7. Judas betrays Jesus with a kiss.
8. Peter cuts off Malchus's ear; Jesus heals it.
9. Jesus arrested; disciples flee.

**Gameplay:** Stealth/tension dungeon — keep disciples awake mini-game. Judas boss encounter. Peter's sword = failed combat; Jesus heals the enemy. Arrest is unavoidable; party scatters. Solo Jesus segment begins.

---

## ARC 13 — THE TRIALS (Matthew 26-27, Mark 14-15, Luke 22-23, John 18-19)
**Location:** Annas's House / Caiaphas / Pilate / Herod / Pilate

**Events:**
1. Before Annas — informal questioning.
2. Before Caiaphas & Sanhedrin — night trial, blasphemy charge.
3. Peter's Three Denials — rooster crows, Peter weeps.
4. Before Pilate (First) — no fault found.
5. Before Herod Antipas — Jesus silent; mocked.
6. Before Pilate (Second) — Barabbas chosen, *"Crucify him!"*
7. Judas's Remorse & Death.
8. Jesus Flogged and Mocked — crown of thorns.

**Gameplay:** Non-combat narrative chapters with interactive events (QTEs, environmental interaction, dialogue choices) to maintain engagement. Peter's denials as emotional cutscenes.

---

## ARC 14 — THE CRUCIFIXION (Matthew 27, Mark 15, Luke 23, John 19)
**Location:** Via Dolorosa / Golgotha

**Events:**
1. Jesus carries his cross.
2. Simon of Cyrene compelled to carry the cross.
3. Women of Jerusalem weep.
4. Crucifixion between two thieves.
5. Seven Last Words from the cross.
6. Darkness from noon to 3 pm.
7. *"It is finished."*
8. Temple curtain tears. Earthquake.
9. Centurion: *"Surely this was the Son of God."*
10. Burial in Joseph of Arimathea's tomb.

**Gameplay:** Somber walking sequence with interactive dialogue events. Non-combat storytelling. Seven Last Words as pivotal dialogue moments. Temple curtain tearing = world-wide visual event. Burial = "game over" style screen before resurrection.

---

## ARC 15 — THE RESURRECTION (Matthew 28, Mark 16, Luke 24, John 20)
**Location:** The Tomb / Jerusalem / Emmaus

**Events:**
1. Empty Tomb — *"He is not here; he has risen!"*
2. Guards bribed.
3. Jesus appears to Mary Magdalene.
4. Road to Emmaus.
5. Jesus appears to the disciples behind locked doors.
6. Thomas's Doubt resolved.
7. Appearance by the Sea of Galilee — miraculous catch.
8. Reinstatement of Peter — *"Do you love me? Feed my sheep."*
9. Various appearances (to 500+).

**Gameplay:** New world tone — everything brighter, enemies changed. Emmaus = travel quest with vanishing stranger. Thomas's doubt stat resolved. Peter's reinstatement mirrors his three denials. Final fishing mini-game.

---

## ARC 16 — THE GREAT COMMISSION & ASCENSION (Matthew 28, Mark 16, Luke 24, Acts 1)
**Location:** Galilee / Jerusalem / Mount of Olives

**Events:**
1. The Great Commission — *"Go and make disciples of all nations."*
2. Final instructions — wait for the Holy Spirit.
3. The Ascension — Jesus taken up into heaven.
4. Two angels: *"He will come back in the same way."*
5. Disciples return to Jerusalem with great joy.

**Gameplay:** Great Commission = final quest objective. Ascension = grand final cutscene. Post-credits scene hints at Pentecost (Acts 2) — potential sequel hook. Credits roll over disciples returning to Jerusalem.

## Design Notes
- Total story arcs: **16**
- Acts 2 (Pentecost) as post-credits scene; potential future expansion
- Each arc = self-contained "chapter" or "town + dungeon" pair
- Scripture references displayed subtly in UI as flavor text
- Game remains reverent and faithful to the biblical text
- Gameplay mechanics are metaphorical — spiritual conflict, faith, and ministry rather than literal violence
